About Me

I am a Master's student in Robotics at Johns Hopkins University, where I work across multiple prestigious labs including CLSP, SARG, Intuitive Computing Lab, and SMARTS Lab. Previously, I completed my triple major in Computer Science, Economics, and Music at UC Berkeley, where I worked with BAIR, Biomimetic Millisystems Lab, and CNMAT.

My research vision is to build AI systems that dramatically amplify human abilities rather than replace them. I believe the future of AI lies not in automation but in augmentation—creating systems that enable people to see, think, create, and act in extraordinary ways. This philosophy drives my work across two main research thrusts:

Culturally-Aware Foundation Models

Making vision-language models respect and represent diverse cultural perspectives, moving beyond Western-centric biases in AI-generated content.

Human-AI Collaboration Systems

Developing agentic frameworks where AI augments domain expertise in high-stakes environments like sports strategy, surgical procedures, and safety operations.
